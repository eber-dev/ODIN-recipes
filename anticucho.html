How to prepare Anticucho

TRADITIONAL PERUVIAN ANTICUCHO

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg (about 2.2 lbs) beef heart (or chicken, if you prefer)
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced or crushed)
  • 3 tablespoons of ají panca paste (Peruvian red chili pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar (or lime juice)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Skewers (if wooden, soak in water for 30 minutes)
  • Boiled potatoes
  • Cooked corn on the cob (Peruvian choclo if available)
  • Spicy sauce (like ají sauce or rocoto cream – optional)

Preparation:

  1. Clean the beef heart thoroughly, removing fat and any tough membranes. Cut it into medium-sized cubes (about 1.5 inches or 3–4 cm).
  2. In a bowl, prepare the marinade: mix the ají panca paste, garlic, vinegar, cumin, salt, and pepper. Optionally, add a bit of vegetable oil.
  3. Add the heart pieces to the marinade. Mix well and let marinate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight in the fridge for deeper flavor.
  4. Thread the marinated meat onto the skewers (about 3–4 pieces per skewer).
  5. Grill the anticuchos over high heat for 2–3 minutes per side, brushing with leftover marinade as they cook to keep them juicy.
  6. Serve hot, with boiled potatoes and corn on the side. Optionally, add a bit of spicy sauce for extra flavor.
