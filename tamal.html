How to prepare Tamal

TRADITIONAL PERUVIAN TAMAL

Ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 500 g (about 1 lb) of peeled white corn (soaked overnight)
  • 1/2 cup lard or vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon ají amarillo (yellow chili pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon ají panca (Peruvian red chili paste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper and cumin to taste
  • Chicken or pork broth, as needed to moisten the dough

For the filling:

  • 500 g (about 1 lb) pork or chicken, cut into small pieces (pre-cooked or sautéed)
  • 1 red onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon ají panca
  • Salt, pepper, and cumin to taste
  • Hard-boiled eggs (sliced)
  • Black olives (pitted)
  • Toasted peanuts (optional)

For wrapping:

  • Banana leaves (softened over open flame or hot water)
  • Kitchen twine to tie

Preparation:

  1. Prepare the dough: Grind the soaked corn into a dough. In a pot, heat the lard or oil and sauté the ají amarillo, ají panca, salt, pepper, and cumin. Add the ground corn and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly. Gradually add broth until the dough becomes soft and thick. Cook for 20–30 minutes until the dough is fully cooked and pliable.
  2. Prepare the filling: In a pan, sauté the onion, garlic, and ají panca. Add the cooked pork or chicken and season with salt, pepper, and cumin. Let it cook for a few minutes until the flavors blend well.
  3. Assemble the tamales: Cut banana leaves into rectangles (about 20x30 cm / 8x12 in). Place a spoonful of dough in the center, add a bit of the filling, a slice of hard-boiled egg, an olive, and peanuts (if using). Add another small spoonful of dough to cover.
  4. Wrap the tamal: Fold the banana leaf into a rectangular packet and tie it with kitchen twine.
  5. Steam the tamales: Place the tamales in a large steamer pot (or a regular pot with a steamer insert). Steam for about 1 hour and 30 minutes.
  6. Serve hot, ideally with salsa criolla (thinly sliced red onion, lime juice, chili, and cilantro).
